The Research Journey

From personal observation to statistical breakthrough

üí° My Initial Observation

I noticed something fascinating: both the stock market and music share fundamental characteristics. They are inherently periodic, exhibiting repeating patterns and cycles. Both demonstrate a range of energy and volume‚Äîmusic through sound intensity and the market through trading activity. Most importantly, both contain recognizable patterns that emerge from seemingly chaotic behavior.

This observation led me to hypothesize: If markets reflect collective human behavior, and music captures fundamental human rhythms, could there be a correlation between jazz improvisational patterns and market movements?

üìã Research Hypothesis & Methodology

Hypothesis: Charlie Parker's bebop jazz patterns, when analyzed through music information retrieval techniques, will correlate with and predict S&P 500 market movements.

Data Extraction Methods:

  • Audio Analysis: Used Librosa library to extract professional audio features at ~43 samples/second
  • Features Extracted: RMS Energy, Spectral Centroid, Spectral Rolloff, Zero Crossing Rate, Onset Strength, Tempo
  • Dataset: 8 Charlie Parker songs, totaling 149,775 data points
  • Market Data: SPY intraday data (1-minute and 5-minute bars) from Yahoo Finance
  • Pattern Matching: Dynamic Time Warping (DTW) algorithm for finding similar shapes
  • Feature Mapping: RMS Energy ‚Üí Market Volume (best correlation found)
PHASE 1: DAILY DATA

Initial Exploration

Compared 8 Charlie Parker songs against 247 days of SPX daily data using correlation analysis.

Result: r = 0.26 (weak correlation)
Lesson: Time scale mismatch - can't compare 3-minute songs to daily data
PHASE 2: BREAKTHROUGH

Intraday Pattern Matching

Switched to 1-minute SPY data (7 days). Used Dynamic Time Warping (DTW) for pattern similarity.

Result: Blue Monk DTW = 0.045, r = 0.987
Discovery: Near-perfect pattern match on Dec 9, 2025 at 14:44
PHASE 3: PREDICTION TEST

5-Minute Forward Returns

Found 25 Blue Monk-like patterns. Tested if they predict next 5 minutes.

Result: 45% win rate (random)
Lesson: 5 minutes dominated by noise
PHASE 4: TIME HORIZONS

Extended Predictions

Tested 5, 10, 20, 30, 45, 60 minute horizons on 7-day dataset.

Result: 60-min shows 58% win rate
Discovery: Au Privave performs best at 70%
PHASE 5: VALIDATION

60-Day Final Test

Validated Au Privave pattern on 60 days of 5-minute SPY data (4,642 bars, 50 patterns).

Result: 72% win rate, +0.0692% avg return
Statistical Tests: T-test p<0.001, Binomial p=0.0013
Conclusion: Statistically significant predictive power ‚úÖ
